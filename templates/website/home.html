An education shaped by faith, discipline, purpose, and the dignity of the girl child

Notre Dame Girls' Academy is owned and managed by the Sisters of Notre Dame de Namur and belongs to a wider educational tradition inspired by St. Julie Billiart, who urged educators to teach children whatever they need for life.

At NDGA, that vision appears through Catholic Gospel values, purposeful learning, pastoral care, boarding structure, and the all-round formation of every girl in our care.

Why Families Choose NDGA

Care, dignity, and a school culture families can trust

Families choose NDGA not only for academics, but for the kind of environment in which girls are protected, known, guided, and helped to grow with confidence.

Child Safeguarding

Every child is recognised as a gift from God.

Child safeguarding remains part of the school's duty of care. NDGA works with parents and guardians to protect student welfare, dignity, and safety in school life, boarding life, and the wider learning environment.

Parent Partnership

Parents and guardians are treated as partners in formation, discipline, communication, and student wellbeing.

Safe Daily Structure
